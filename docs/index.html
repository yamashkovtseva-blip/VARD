Frequently asked

Is Vard really free? +
Yes. Source-available under Business Source License 1.1; converts to Apache 2.0 on 2030-01-01. Free for internal use, forever.
What am I paying for if I run it on my own hardware? +
The commercial license and the closed-source Pro / Enterprise features — project RBAC, audit log, SSO, custom connectors, priority support. Same open-core model as Sentry, GitLab, CockroachDB. Your data, your AI, and your hardware stay yours; you never pay us for hosting.
How does Community differ from Self-hosted Pro? +
Community is free under BSL for internal use, up to 5 users, with community support. Pro adds project-level RBAC, audit log, notebook versioning, priority email support, and removes the seat limit (sold from 5 seats up). Both run entirely on your infrastructure.
Can I use my own LLM (OpenAI, Claude, local)? +
Yes. Ollama by default; OpenAI-compatible API supported.
What databases do you support? +
Postgres today. DuckDB, Snowflake, BigQuery, Redshift, ClickHouse on the roadmap (open in GitHub Discussions).
Is my data sent anywhere? +
No. Vard runs entirely on your machines — even the AI is local.
Do you have SOC 2? +
In progress. Target Q3 2026. We help Enterprise customers with their own audits.
How is Vard different from Briefer / Hex / Jupyter? +
See the comparison table above. Short version: only Vard combines self-hosting, local AI, and real-time collab in one product.
